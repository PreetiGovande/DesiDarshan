1. Breweries, Chuwarak, The Most Famous Drink of Tripura:

Location: Laxmilunga, Tripura. Origin Story: Renowned for its cultural tapestry, Tripura is equally famed for its potent alcoholic concoctions that surpass many sophisticated drinks. In the realm of the 14 gods, a common local recommendation for a drink is none other than Chuwarak. This traditional beverage not only packs a punch but also boasts a rich profile of pro- and pre-biotics, courtesy of its intricate fermentation process. Ask anyone in this diverse land, and they're likely to present you with a glass of Chuwarak, a testament to the region's unique and flavorful drinking traditions. Uniqueness: Chuwarak, a distinctive white beverage crafted in Tripura, undergoes a unique preparation process using an array of raw materials, including jackfruit, rice, and pineapple. The infusion of local ingredients such as Thakotor leaves and Tokhiseleng leaves adds a unique touch to its composition. Once savored, this drink becomes irresistible, compelling those who experience its special aroma and comforting taste to indulge in it again and again. Price Range: ₹150 ₹600 (approx.)

5. Hidden Gems Neermahal, Melaghar:

Location: Neermahal is located in the middle of Rudrasagar Lake, near Melaghar. Address: G848+J76, Rudijala, Tripura 799115. Phone: 1800 4254 5454 Origin Story: Constructed in 1930 by Maharaja Bir Bikram Kishore Manikya, Neermahal translates to "Water Palace." It was designed to be a summer retreat and a cultural center, showcasing the fusion of Hindu and Islamic architectural styles. Uniqueness: Neermahal is the only water palace in Eastern India, and its architecture and scenic location make it a hidden gem. The palace hosts the Neermahal Water Festival, celebrating the cultural heritage of Tripura. Price Range: Entry fee for the palace: INR 80 Entry fee for a boat ride: INR 20 Ferry or boat fare: INR 50 per person Palace entry fee: INR 30

Where to Buy: Tickets available at the entrance.

8. Annual Festivals Kharchi Puja, Agartala:

Location: Celebrated at the Chaturdasha Devata Temple, Old Agartala. Origin Story: Kharchi Puja is a centuries-old festival dedicated to Chaturdasha Devata, the fourteen gods of the Tripuri community. It is celebrated to cleanse and rejuvenate the deities, symbolizing the renewal of life. Uniqueness: Kharchi Puja is one of the most significant festivals in Tripura, attracting devotees and tourists alike. The rituals, processions, and cultural performances during the festival showcase the state's religious and cultural diversity. Price Range: Free entry for most events.
